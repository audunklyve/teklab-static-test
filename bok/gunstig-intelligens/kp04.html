    4. Kunstig intelligens om gunstig og ugunstig intelligens⚓︎

    GPT-3 fikk fem små oppgaver av forfatteren for å beskrive fordeler og ulemper ved bruk av KI i redaksjoner.

    Poenget med oppgavene var å se hvordan GPT-3 generer tekst om samme emne som vi selv prøver å beskrive. På sekunder ble det generert tekster som inneholder det samme som vi har brukt måneder på å finne ut av. Alle hovedargumenter for og mot er på plass. Men en som kjenner norsk medievirkelighet vil se at GPT-3 baserer seg på en britisk eller amerikansk medievirkelighet. Oppgavene ble gitt før GPT ble tilgjengelig på norsk.

    Oppgavene ble gitt GPT-3. Text-davinci-001, via OpenAI, Playground 7. Februar 2022. De autogenererte tekstene er ikke redigert eller endret.

    Oppgave 1 til GPT-3: Write an article about the benefits of Artificial intelligence in the news room.⚓︎

    Artificial intelligence has been making waves in the business world for a few years now, and its potential applications in the newsroom are just starting to be explored. Already, AI has been used to help reporters gather data and compile stories, and some believe that its potential for automating tasks and improving efficiency.

    1. Increased accuracy and efficiency

    Artificial intelligence can help newsrooms to be more accurate and efficient in their reporting. By automating the process of fact checking and data analysis, AI can help to reduce the number of errors made in news articles.

    2. Improved customer service

    AI can also help to improve customer service in newsrooms. By automatically responding to customer queries and complaints, AI can help to speed up the customer service process and improve customer satisfaction.

    3. Enhanced research capabilities

    AI can also help to enhance research capabilities in newsrooms. By automatically gathering and analysing data from a variety of sources, AI can help to speed up the research process and improve the quality of research.

    4. Increased productivity

    AI can help to increase productivity in newsrooms by automating tasks such as data entry and transcription. This can help journalists to focus on more important tasks and improve the overall quality of news reporting.

    5. Improved decision making

    AI can also help to improve decision making in newsrooms. By analysing data and providing insights into how readers are responding to news articles, AI can help journalists to make better decisions about what content to publish.

    Oppgave 2 til GPT-3: Write an article about the down sides of Artificial intelligence in the news room⚓︎

    Artificial intelligence has been making waves in the newsroom, with some heralding it as the savior of journalism and others warning of its dangers. But what are the potential downsides of using AI in the newsroom?

    First, there is the risk of bias. AI can be biased if the data it is trained on is biased. For example, if an AI is trained on a dataset that is biased against women, it will be more likely to produce results that are biased against women.

    Second, there is the risk of inaccuracy. AI can make mistakes if the data it is working with is inaccurate. For example, if an AI is used to automatically generate headlines, it may produce inaccurate headlines if the data it is working with is inaccurate.

    Third, there is the risk of censorship. AI can be used to censor content if it is given the authority to do so. For example, if an AI is used to scan social media for content that is deemed inappropriate, it may censor content that is not actually inappropriate.

    Fourth, there is the risk of automation. AI can be used to automate tasks that used to be done by human beings. For example, an AI can be used to automatically generate stories based on data. This could lead to job losses for journalists.

    Fifth, there is the risk of manipulation. AI can be used to manipulate content if it is given the authority to do so. For example, if an AI is used to scan social media for content, it could be used to manipulate content by deleting or altering posts.

    Finally, there is the risk of privacy invasion. AI can be used to invade people’s privacy by collecting and analyzing their data. For example, if an AI is used to scan social media for content, it could collect data about people’s likes and dislikes.

Second, AI can help journalists to better understand and report on complex topics. For example, AI can be used to create data visualizations that help journalists to understand how, for example, the economy is performing. Finally, AI can be used to identify potential stories and sources. For example, AI can scan social media and other online sources for potential stories.

    Oppgave 4 til GPT-3: Why should journalists fear AI?⚓︎

    There are a few reasons why journalists should fear AI. First, AI can automate certain tasks that journalists currently do, such as data gathering and fact checking. This could lead to journalists losing their jobs, or having their roles diminished. Second, AI can be used to create fake news, which could further erode trust in the media. Finally, AI can be used to track and monitor journalists, which could lead to censorship or intimidation.

    There is no easy answer to this question. On the one hand, artificial intelligence can help reporters and newsrooms by automating tasks such as fact-checking, data analysis, and transcription. This can free up reporters to focus on more important tasks, such as investigating stories and interviewing sources. On the other hand, there is a risk that AI could replace reporters altogether, leading to a decline in the quality of the news.

    Overall, it is likely that AI will be beneficial to reporters and the newsroom in the long run. However, there will likely be some challenges in the transition period, and it is important to be aware of the risks and benefits of using AI.
